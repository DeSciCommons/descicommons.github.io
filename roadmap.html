DeSciCommons Roadmap

Our journey to transform scientific research through decentralization

Current Status

We are preparing to launch the DeSciCommons definition process

Phase 0

Timeline

Phase 0: Foundation

Q2 2025 - Q3 2025

Status: In Progress

  • Launch DeSciCommons website
  • Build initial community
  • Establish communication channels
  • Form core working groups

Phase 1: Discovery

Q4 2025

Goal: Understand the landscape

  • Survey existing DeSci projects
  • Identify common challenges
  • Gather requirements from stakeholders
  • Map technical infrastructure needs

Phase 2: Design

Q1 2026

Goal: Create initial standards

  • Working groups draft proposals
  • Define governance frameworks
  • Establish data standards
  • Design incentive mechanisms

Phase 3: Feedback

Q2 2026

Goal: Refine through community input

  • Open proposals for public comment
  • Host community workshops
  • Iterate based on feedback
  • Build consensus on key standards

Phase 4: Pilot

Q3 2026

Goal: Test in real projects

  • Select pilot Research DAOs
  • Implement standards in practice
  • Document lessons learned
  • Refine based on experience

Phase 5: Launch

Q4 2026

Goal: Official DeSciCommons v1.0

  • Publish final standards
  • Launch certification program
  • Release implementation tools
  • Begin broader adoption

Phase 6: Scale

Q1 2027 and beyond

Goal: Ecosystem growth

  • Onboard major Research DAOs
  • Expand to new scientific domains
  • Continuous improvement process
  • Build global DeSci infrastructure

Key Milestones

Near Term (2025-2026)

  • 100+ community members
  • 5 working groups established
  • First draft standards published
  • 3 pilot Research DAOs committed

Long Term (2027+)

  • $100M+ managed by DeSci-compliant DAOs
  • 1000+ researchers participating
  • Integration with major research tools
  • Recognition by traditional funders

Success Metrics

We'll measure our progress through:

  • Adoption: Number of projects implementing DeSciCommons standards
  • Funding: Total research funding flowing through compliant DAOs
  • Quality: Research output metrics and reproducibility rates
  • Community: Active participants and contributor growth
  • Impact: Scientific breakthroughs enabled by DeSci infrastructure

Our Vision for 2030

By 2030, we envision a scientific ecosystem where:

  • Researchers spend 80% of their time on actual research (vs. 40% today)
  • Any qualified researcher can access funding based on merit alone
  • All research data and outputs are openly accessible and verifiable
  • Collaboration across institutions and borders is seamless
  • Scientific breakthroughs happen faster due to reduced friction

Together, we're building the future of science.
